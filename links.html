Links to Things I Like

Life Style Things

- /r/fitness is one subreddit I like a lot on Reddit. I personally think fitness is something that many people still neglect. Me personally, I think that a balance of lifting, diet, and personal habits go a long way towards living a good life.

- /r/AskAnAmerican is another subreddit I like, simply because it's nice to look at other people's perspectives on American life. Good laughs, and also surprisingly educational.

Leisure and Hobby Things

- Core-A Gaming is a YouTube channel that I watch a lot, because I enjoy fighting games like Super Smash Bros. and Street Fighter. They explain certain fighting game concepts very well, like "footsies" and "frame trapping" etc. It's fun to listen to, even if you don't know much about fighting games.

Educational Things

- stack overflow is quite possible one of the greatest things mankind has created aside from Reddit, although saying that may indicate I go on Reddit way too much. The holy grail of general questions and answers for coding, I would be very surprised if you haven't heard of this website if you're looking at my website. Either way, if you start coding, this will probably be the website you'll go to many many times.
